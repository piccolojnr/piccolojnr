## Full bio

### Background

Daud started through the ALX Software Engineering Programme (Cohort 17), a 12-month curriculum that goes from low-level C through to modern web frameworks. He completed it while building client products and shipping production work at the same time.

### What I do

Daud builds full-stack web applications, SaaS products, and IoT systems, primarily for clients in Ghana. His clients include large enterprises (KGL Group, Allied Ghana), a university SRC, and a startup. He typically handles everything solo: requirements, architecture, deployment, and ongoing maintenance.

### How I work

Daud takes a brief, figures out the architecture, builds it, and keeps it running. He's used to ambiguous requirements and difficult clients; most of his projects involve both. Delivering solo has meant getting good at scoping, client communication, and coordinating with designers, networking engineers, and other collaborators when needed.

### Recent focus

Over the last year, Daud has moved well beyond web development. He built a full IoT laundry kiosk system (hardware integration, Android kiosk app, backend API, admin dashboard) and went deep on DevOps, Linux server administration, and CI/CD pipelines. Most recently he designed and shipped Memraiq, an AI SaaS platform with a custom RAG pipeline covering semantic chunking, Qdrant vector search, Neo4j graph retrieval, query classification, and cross-encoder reranking. He built it entirely solo across four services and three live deployments. He's now developing the next version, which replaces the initial third-party RAG dependency with his own system.

### Beyond work

Daud is interested in using technology for practical problems in the Ghanaian and African context, particularly for small businesses and institutions that rarely get purpose-built software.
